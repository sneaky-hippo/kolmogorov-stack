How the autopilot finds savings — the honest version

  1. Observe. Every prompt that flows through the wrapper is hashed by template-signature (the leading instruction, with quoted strings and numbers normalized). The bodies are PII-redacted; we never store raw user content.
  2. Cluster. When the same template hits 4+ times, we mark it as a synthesis candidate. Most clusters are classification, intent detection, format checking, language detection — the boring 80%.
  3. Verify before we replace. When you click synthesize, we mint a deterministic JS function and compare its output against the LLM responses we recorded. We require ≥95% match before we'd ever route to it.
  4. Route silently. The wrapper transparently sends future matching calls to the recipe. Misses fall through to your original LLM call — nothing breaks.
  5. Watch for drift. The autopilot keeps shadow-LLM-calling at low rate. If outputs diverge above threshold, the recipe demotes itself and re-synthesizes.
