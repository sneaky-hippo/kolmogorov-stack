## The competitive landscape — and the gap

Every player in clinical AI today is selling **hosted SaaS**: Hippocratic AI
(agents), Abridge / Nabla / Suki / Dragon Copilot / DeepScribe (ambient
scribes), Glass / OpenEvidence (CDS), Epic Cosmos (in-EHR predictive),
Augmedix / Commure (hybrid scribe), Iterative Health (clinical trials),
Regard / Notable / Tennr (RCM + ops), Eleos (behavioral health), Slingshot
(consumer mental health).

**No one credibly says:** "you compile your own model, you own the binary,
you own the receipts, the data never crosses your firewall." That's the
unfilled slot. kolm's healthcare positioning lives there.

## Eight defensible positioning gaps (ranked)

1. **Your hospital's AI, not the vendor's** — every player above is hosted SaaS.
2. **Architectural privacy, not policy privacy** — no outbound socket vs "HIPAA-compliant" promises.
3. **Verifiable behavior, not vendor promises** — signed K-score receipts per artifact.
4. **Compile, don't subscribe** — one .kolm file vs per-seat-per-month.
5. **Air-gapped as a first-class mode** — VA, IHS, DoD-adjacent.
6. **Specialty-by-specialty compile-your-own** — rad-onc, EP cardiology, NICU, transplant.
7. **Bring-your-own-model neutrality** — Llama / Mistral / Qwen / future open weights.
8. **Compliance-officer-verifiable receipts** — HMAC-signed eval evidence sign-off-able BEFORE go-live.
