Response distillation

Response distillation, sometimes called hard-label distillation or sequence-level KD, trains the student on the teacher's sampled responses with plain cross-entropy. No logit access required, works across tokenizer families, runs on cheap APIs. The trade-off is the dark-knowledge signal you lose by collapsing the teacher's full distribution to a single token at each step.

What it is

Kim and Rush (2016) formalized sequence-level knowledge distillation: the teacher samples (or beam-searches) a response, and the student is trained on it with standard cross-entropy. This is the special case of soft-label distillation where the temperature has effectively gone to zero and only the argmax token at each position remains. The advantages are operational: no logit-level API access is needed, so the teacher can be any closed-source API; the student and teacher tokenizers do not need to align; the training loop is identical to SFT on any other labelled corpus. The disadvantage, named by Hinton et al. (2015) as the loss of "dark knowledge", is that the student sees the second and third-most-likely tokens nowhere; the teacher's confidence calibration is not transferred. For the typical kolm gap (a 14B teacher to a 3B student on a narrow task) the loss is small; for cross-family transfer with a large capacity gap (70B to 3B across families) the loss is more material and on-policy or rationale variants help.

How kolm uses it

Response distillation is the default training mode in kolm v0.2.6. The synthesis pipeline samples teacher responses for each row in the corpus (with the cache-control hint enabled for cost; see /research/prompt-cache), records them in the capture log with the prompt and the teacher id, and the trainer runs cross-entropy on the response tokens against the student's logits. The full token-level KD path (forward-KL, reverse-KL, JSD) is documented separately at /research/response-distillation and is enabled when the teacher exposes logits and shares the student's tokenizer; otherwise the trainer falls back to response distillation. The receipt records the teacher id, the sampled-response hash, the corpus row count, and the post-train holdout K-score.
