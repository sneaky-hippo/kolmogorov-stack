LLM-as-judge

An LLM-as-judge is a model that reads a prompt and one or more candidate responses and emits a score, a preference, or a rubric breakdown. It is the cheapest available proxy for human evaluation on open-ended tasks. It is also biased in well-documented ways: it prefers the first response in a pair, longer responses, and responses from its own model family. Kolm mitigates by running a mixture of three independent judges and taking the median.

What it is

Zheng et al. (2023) formalized LLM-as-judge with MT-Bench: GPT-4 reads two model responses and picks one or declares a tie, with chain-of-thought reasoning before the verdict. The same paper documented position bias (the model prefers the first listed response by ~10 points), verbosity bias (longer responses win), and self-enhancement bias (a judge model rates its own family higher). Follow-up work on dedicated judge models tries to remove the API cost and the bias: PandaLM (Wang et al., 2024) and JudgeLM (Zhu et al., 2024) train smaller open-weight judges via SFT on GPT-4 preferences. Prometheus (Kim et al., 2024) trains a fine-grained rubric-following judge that can score on a 1 to 5 Likert scale with explicit criteria. G-Eval (Liu et al., 2023) uses chain-of-thought with form-filling to reduce variance. None of them eliminate the underlying biases; the published recipe is randomized position, length normalization, and a panel of multiple judges with disagreement reporting.

How kolm uses it

The K-score gate in v0.2.6 runs a panel of three judges per holdout row when the task is open-ended (no verifiable reward function). The judges are configurable; the default panel rotates across two frontier models from different families plus one open-weight Prometheus-style scorer to break ties. Each row's three scores are reduced to a median; the standard deviation is recorded in the receipt as a disagreement signal. Position is randomized per pair. Length-normalization is applied for tasks where verbosity is not desired. Self-enhancement is mitigated by never including the student's own family as a judge. Dedicated open-weight judge training (PandaLM, JudgeLM) is not currently offered as a kolm pipeline; it is planned for v0.3 as a marketplace recipe class.
