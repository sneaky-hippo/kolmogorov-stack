Rationale distillation

Rationale distillation gives the student both the teacher's final answer and the teacher's reasoning chain as supervision. The student learns to produce the same reasoning before the same answer. Out-of-distribution generalization improves; response length and training cost both rise.

What it is

Standard response distillation (/research/response-distill) teaches the student to mimic the teacher's final response. For tasks that benefit from intermediate computation, the final response alone is a thin signal: the student sees the answer but not how to reach it. Rationale distillation, also called chain-of-thought distillation (Magister et al. 2023, Teaching Small Language Models to Reason) or reasoning distillation (Hsieh et al. 2023, Distilling Step-by-Step), records the teacher's chain of thought along with the answer and uses both as training targets. The student is trained to emit the reasoning first, then the answer. Empirical results: a 770M T5 distilled with rationales matches a 540B PaLM teacher on commonsense QA at a fraction of the inference cost. The cost is longer responses (4 to 10x more output tokens) and more training tokens per row. The Orca line (Mukherjee et al. 2023, Mitra et al. 2023) productionized the approach at scale by also giving the teacher detailed system prompts that elicit higher-quality rationales.

How kolm uses it

Rationale distillation is not implemented in kolm v0.2.6. The shipped distillation stage trains on response only; the capture proxy does not currently extract and store the teacher's chain of thought as a separate field. The integration is on the v0.3 roadmap: when enabled per recipe (distill.with_rationale: true), the synthesis pipeline will prompt the teacher with a published rationale-eliciting wrapper (Orca-style system prompt), parse the response into (rationale, answer), store both, and train the student to emit them in sequence. The K-score gate will score the answer field only; the rationale is supervision, not test material. The receipt will record whether rationale supervision was used, the rationale-to-answer length ratio, and a held-out rationale-grounding score (judge-rated, opt-in).
