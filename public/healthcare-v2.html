01The pain

Why your team blocks
the procurement form.

Concrete failure modes our healthcare design partners ran into trying to use frontier APIs on clinical workloads.

PHI egress

PHI cannot leave the premises.

Every prompt to a frontier API is a BAA-scoped disclosure event. kolm runs entirely inside the hospital network boundary your auditor already trusts.

BAA review

HIPAA BAA on hyperscalers is hard.

Subprocessor lists run 200 deep. Each addition triggers a contract review. kolm ships as one signed artifact, no subprocessors at runtime.

Vendor lock

Model deprecation breaks clinical workflows.

14-day deprecation notices are not compatible with annual clinical sign-off. kolm pins the model into the artifact for the life of the deployment.

02Architecture

Capture, compile, ship.
All inside your VPC.

Three nodes inside the hospital boundary. Zero nodes outside. The kolm CLI is the only thing that crosses the airlock, and only at compile time, not at run time.

04Reference recipes

Two signed .kolm
starting artifacts.

Fork either against your de-identified gold set. Each ships with a K-score, a verifier, and a receipt chain your auditor can re-verify byte-identically.

phi-redactor.kolm

Strips 18 HIPAA Safe Harbor identifiers from free-text clinical notes before downstream processing. Verifier penalizes any token resembling a phone, MRN, DOB, or SSN.

Download from registry →
uscdi-extract.kolm

Extracts USCDI v3 data elements (problems, allergies, medications, vitals) from unstructured note text. Output is JSON keyed to USCDI codes. On-prem only.
