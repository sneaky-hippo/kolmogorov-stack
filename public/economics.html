The hardware unlock — where the registry deploys

Once a recipe is compiled, it is a deterministic function. It does not need a model. It does not need a GPU. It does not need network. The registry — the public catalog of every published recipe — becomes a portable object that can be installed anywhere.

A device with the registry installed has the structured-task behavior of every frontier model that ever published a recipe to it. Forever. Offline. For free.

We are not making the model smaller. We are decomposing the model's behavior into deterministic primitives that no longer need the model. That is a different operation than quantization, distillation, or pruning. It is closer to "compiling Python to machine code" than "compressing a JPEG."

The internet rewired

Today, every structured AI call in the world — classify, route, extract, validate — traverses the internet to a handful of GPU clusters and back. It is the largest single source of new datacenter traffic, and the largest single AI cost line for every company building on LLMs.

Once the recipe registry exists, that traffic disappears. Not because the work disappears — the work runs locally. The registry is the compiled, distributable artifact of the work the LLM used to do. Every structured call that becomes a recipe is one less call humanity needs.

Historical analogue · CDNs

In 1998, every static asset on the internet was served by an origin server. By 2008, ~60% of web traffic was edge-cached. The origin-to-edge shift didn't reduce the cost of an asset; it changed where the asset lived. The internet kept growing — the work just moved closer to the user.

Recipe is the same shift, one layer up. Static AI behavior moves from origin (datacenter LLM) to edge (the device). The dynamic residual — open-ended generation, long-context reasoning, the genuinely creative tail — stays at the origin. That residual is what frontier labs are best at. Everyone wins.

What this means for the AI bill of materials

Today: ~80% LLM-API spend is repeat structured calls. ~20% is open-ended generation.

After Recipe: ~80% of structured calls served locally for free. API spend collapses to the ~20% that should never have been deterministic in the first place. That ~20% is what Anthropic and OpenAI should be selling. The other ~80% was always a category error.

The compounding registry

Every recipe published to the registry covers one structured task. A registry of N recipes covers N tasks. Behavioral coverage compounds with the size of the registry.

The registry is open. The spec is MIT-licensed. Self-hosting is one Node binary. We are not the registry — we are a reference implementation. The natural endpoint is the same as npm or the JVM: a public good with thousands of contributors and a long tail of forks.

For the operator: every recipe you publish makes the next user's first call free. For the user: every new contributor's recipe is one fewer call you have to pay for. Network effect, with the math of generator-verifier asymmetry as the substrate.
