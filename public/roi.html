What's in the math, what isn't.

In: per-call frontier cost (input + output), ~8% egress + variance buffer, kolm plan, teacher cost (k · rows · tokens at same per-token price as production), recompile cadence over 3 years.

Out: engineer time, GPU rental during fine-tune (Pro/Teams handles this on the bridge), one-time recall index population, and the cost of not compiling (cloud variance, prompt regressions, rate-limit incidents, vendor revocation risk).

Assumption: kolm fits tasks 80%+ deterministic on labeled examples. For free-form generation, see /k-score. Numbers are unrounded; we do not multiply by hand-wavy "value" factors.

Sensitivity: Even if frontier prices drop 30% in 18 months, kolm still wins on year-3 cumulative for any workload above ~50K calls/month at typical token mixes. Toggle the inputs above to verify.
