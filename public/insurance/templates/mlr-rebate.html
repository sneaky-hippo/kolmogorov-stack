MLR rebate calculator (PPACA §2718)

A kolm template that computes the Medical Loss Ratio (MLR) under PPACA §2718 and the resulting member rebate, if any. Class: rule (class 0). Input: { reporting_year, market: "individual"|"small_group"|"large_group", state, premium_revenue, incurred_claims, quality_improvement_expense, taxes_fees, member_months }. Output: { mlr_numerator, mlr_denominator, mlr_ratio, threshold, rebate_owed_total, per_member_rebate }. Thresholds: 80% individual + small group; 85% large group.

recipe-class: rule (class 0) spec: PPACA §2718 + 45 CFR §158 law: 42 U.S.C. 300gg-18

Input & output spec

One MLR computation per (reporting_year, market, state) aggregation block. Input is the annual P&L roll-up; output is the ratio, the threshold for that market segment, and the rebate owed if the ratio falls below the floor.

Statutory thresholds

The MLR floor varies by market segment. 45 CFR §158.211 pins the cut points; the rule library encodes them so QA can re-derive any rebate calc without re-reading the regulation.

individual market
≥ 80% MLR
small group market
≥ 80% MLR
large group market
≥ 85% MLR

Formula per 45 CFR §158.221: MLR = (incurred_claims + quality_improvement_expense) / (premium_revenue − taxes_fees). When the ratio falls below the segment floor, the rebate per 45 CFR §158.240 equals (threshold − MLR) × (premium_revenue − taxes_fees), distributed pro rata across member months.

4-ring receipt schema

Every MLR computation ships a receipt your auditor can re-verify offline. The chain proves which aggregation block was ingested, which version of the rule library was applied, the numerator and denominator that resulted, and the seal.

ring 01 · pre-image
market + state + reporting_year + p&l_hash
tenant-rotated HMAC
ring 02 · derived
threshold_table_hash + regulation_version
bound to recipe_version
ring 03 · execution
mlr_ratio + rebate_owed_total
deterministic, no judge
ring 04 · seal
SHA-256(ring1..3) · verify endpoint
offline-verifiable

Verify command: kolm verify --hash <receipt_hash>. Runs locally against the seal, no network required.
