How it works · 8 stages · gather to sign

Compile, end-to-end.

Eight stages: gather, spec, synthesize, k-sample, verify, fit, observe, sign. Frontier as teacher, artifact as student, receipt as audit row. Reads like a paper because the only credible answer to "how does it work" is one an engineer can implement.

What this page describes vs. what v0.2 ships today

The 8-stage pipeline below is the full design. The v0.2 build ships stages 1, 2, 3, 5, 7, and 8 as rule-class compilation: gather, spec, synthesize a deterministic recipe pack, verify against the held-out 20%, observe and tighten coverage, and sign the artifact. Plus the wave-144 additions: MoE composition, the pure-JS byte-level BPE tokenizer, kolm extract, and kolm doc check. Stages 4 (k-sample from a frontier teacher), 6 (LoRA fit), and the INT4/GGUF/ONNX bundling referenced lower on this page are on the product gates list, not in this build. We left the eight-stage write-up intact because it is the target architecture; treat any LoRA or INT4 paragraph below as a roadmap description, not a feature claim about this release.

Eight stages. Frontier as teacher. Artifact as student. Receipt as audit row.

01

Gather: collect the gold examples and the corpus.

30–200 input/output pairs (gold set) plus a directory of related documents (corpus). 20% of the gold is held out for eval and never seen during synthesis or training.

Inputs

  • examples.jsonl - one input/output pair per line
  • ./corpus/ - text, PDFs, images, audio, video. Auto-detected (magika), routed to the right embedder
  • policy.md (optional) - refusals, formatting, regulatory language
  • frontier API key - Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or your own; used as the teacher

What the compiler does

  • parses, dedupes, normalizes encoding
  • 80/20 train/eval split, deterministic seed
  • indexes corpus into a tenant-scoped sqlite-vec namespace
  • writes deterministic data_hash into the manifest
02

Spec: derive the verifier from the examples.

A task is compilable only if there's a deterministic check on the output. The compiler synthesizes one from your examples: JSON schema, regex, custom function, or a learned classifier for free-form output. This is what makes the K-score quantitative.

Verifier types

  • schema - JSON Schema synthesized from output shape (most common)
  • regex - for structured strings (IDs, claim numbers, redactions)
  • function - user-supplied verify.js or verify.py
  • classifier - a small learned head (logistic regression on frontier embeddings) for free-form output, trained on gold
  • composite - AND/OR combinations

Output

verifier.json - deterministic, hashed, embedded in the artifact.
score(input, output) -> [0,1]
the K-score is the mean over the held-out eval set.
03

Synthesize: draft the recipe pack from the examples.

Most tokens in structured tasks are deterministic given the prefix. The compiler emits a recipe pack indexed by prefix shape and recalled-context embedding. At run time, recipes draft boring tokens; the LoRA fills the substantive ones. The speculative-decoding mechanism.

What ships in the pack

  • prefix-shape entries - e.g., {"triage": always followed by "urgent"|"routine"|"escalate"
  • embedding-shape entries - e.g., recalled context resembling "ER intake form" pins field order
  • fallback - if no recipe matches, the LoRA decodes from scratch (no quality cost)
04

k-sample: query the frontier as the teacher.

For each training example, the compiler asks the frontier k times (default k=4). The verifier scores all k; the winner advances. Labels are "what the frontier said that the verifier accepts," not "what the frontier said."

Why k-sampling instead of one call

Single frontier calls are unreliable on the long tail. k=4 with deterministic verification reaches ≥0.95 label correctness on most structured tasks. Cost is 4× teacher spend per training example, amortized to zero after compile.

Recall injection

If a corpus is provided, every k-sample request is grounded in top-k recalled chunks retrieved by embedding similarity.

05

verify: the deterministic gate.

Every k-sampled output runs through the verifier. If no sample passes, the example is dropped (better to train on less than on a wrong label). The fraction passing is the label coverage, recorded in the manifest.

Failure handling

  • if <80% of examples produce passing samples, the compile pauses and asks for more or higher-quality gold
  • if the frontier consistently fails the verifier, you get a "frontier cannot do this task at this gate" error - compile fails fast, re-spec the task
06

fit: train the LoRA.

Verified labels train a LoRA delta over the chosen base. Training runs server-side or on a customer-hosted bridge on a single H100 / 5090; 8–25 minutes for a 3B-class base.

Hyperparameters & bases

basesQwen 2.5 / 3.x (1B, 3B, 7B, 14B), Llama-3 8B, Phi-3 3.8B, Hermes-3 8B, Gemma 2 9B. All open, all INT4-quantizable.
LoRA rank16 default, 32 for high-coverage. Adapter ≤ 100MB at rank 16 on a 7B base.
quantizationINT4 GGUF (CPU/phone) and bf16 (GPU), both produced per compile.
training3-5 epochs, bf16 mixed-precision, gradient checkpointing on H100 or 5090.

What "Hermes-class on your task" actually means

Not 3B-with-LoRA matching 70B raw on arbitrary inputs. On the task you compiled (your eval set, your input distribution), the artifact achieves ≥K-score-floor accuracy. The eval set is held out; you can re-run it offline anytime.

07

observe: harden the recipe pack from the trained model.

After training, the compiler runs the LoRA across the eval set with verbose logging. Tokens emitted at ≥99% confidence and predicted by the pack are confirmed; disagreements are removed; high-confidence tokens not in the pack are candidates.

This is the compounding loop

Every public compile feeds candidates to the public registry; each growth shrinks the next observation budget; smaller models draft faster; compiles get cheaper.

08

sign: bundle and seal.

Bundles base-model pointer + behavior pack + recipes + verifier + lookup index + manifest into a signed zip. Signature is HMAC-SHA256 over the canonical hash chain. v0.1 receipts are HMAC-verifiable by the issuer or any holder of the shared tenant receipt secret; Ed25519 public-key receipts are a roadmap tier.

What it means at the receiving end

  • every output ships with a per-call receipt over (input_hash, output_hash, manifest_sha)
  • any holder of the tenant receipt secret (you, your auditor under NDA, kolm support) can re-verify offline; Ed25519 public-key receipts are roadmap
  • tampering invalidates the signature; the runtime refuses to load
  • frontier drift has no effect - the artifact is sealed and reproducible
09Properties

Why this shape produces what it does.

Small (3B + 100MB LoRA + 10MB recipes ≈ 2–3GB), portable (phone, laptop, edge), reproducible (sealed manifest), auditable (HMAC per output), offline-first. Trade-off: no generalization beyond the compiled task; K-score is the accuracy contract.

What it is not

  • not a quantized 70B on a phone - it's a 3B-class artifact that behaves 70B-class on the compiled task
  • not memory or context-management - corpus is grounding, not personality
  • not a frontier wrapper - frontier is compile-time only; runtime is local
