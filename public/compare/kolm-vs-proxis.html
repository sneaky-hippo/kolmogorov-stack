kolm  /  compare  /  kolm vs Proxis (YC W26)

Proxis distills fast. kolm signs and ships the result.

Proxis (Y Combinator W26 batch) is building developer-friendly knowledge distillation tooling: take a frontier teacher model, generate a synthetic dataset, train a small student, get measurable cost and latency wins. That is a real and useful slice of the workflow. kolm sits on the layer above: it consumes the distilled student weights, gates them on a K-score eval, wraps them in a signed manifest with a full receipt chain, and emits a portable .kolm file. The two products are complementary far more often than competitive.

Wave 274 · reviewed 2026-05-18 · distillation tooling vs signed compiled artifact

What Proxis ships in 2026.

  • Knowledge distillation pipeline. Teacher selection, synthetic dataset generation, student training, evaluation loop, all wrapped in a developer-grade CLI/SDK.
  • Cost and latency optimization story. Replace expensive frontier API calls with a smaller student that hits similar task accuracy at a fraction of the per-token cost.
  • YC W26 momentum. Strong founding team, fresh seed capital, broad early adoption across mid-stage AI startups doing inference-cost optimization.
  • Developer ergonomics first. The product is shaped for an ML engineer running iterations, not for a procurement officer reading a receipt chain.

What Proxis does not (yet) ship: a signed portable file you mail to a phone, a cryptographically verifiable build receipt that an auditor can read against an external transparency log, a PHI redactor wired into the teacher call, or a deployment mode that runs air-gapped. Those are kolm's surface.

The stack with both.

Proxis runs the distillation iteration loop and produces strong student weights. kolm takes those weights, runs the K-score eval gate, captures the full provenance (teacher, redactor, train split, holdout, recipes), signs the resulting artifact with Ed25519, posts a Rekor transparency entry, and writes the portable .kolm file your runtime consumes. The split is clean:

task brief→Proxis→distilled student weights→kolm→signed .kolm + receipt chain→runtime

The signed .kolm is the contract between the distillation team and the deploy team. Proxis can optimize the inner loop aggressively; kolm carries the cryptographic provenance and eval gate that compliance and procurement need.

The Proxis + kolm integration in one CLI session.

The cleanest pairing: Proxis runs the distillation iteration loop and emits student weights. You point kolm compile --from-weights at the Proxis output, attach the captured I/O pairs as seeds, and kolm produces the signed .kolm file your runtime and auditor consume. 
# 1. Distill with Proxis (their tooling, their loop)
$ proxis distill task.yaml \
    --teacher=anthropic:claude-opus-4-7 \
    --student=qwen2.5-7b \
    --out ./student-weights

  WROTE student-weights/*.safetensors + metrics.json

# 2. Wrap the Proxis output with kolm
$ kolm compile --from-weights ./student-weights \
    --seeds ./captures.jsonl \
    --teacher=anthropic:claude-opus-4-7 \
    --redact=phi --eval-gate=0.85

  PASS  K-score 0.914 (A=0.93 T=0.92 F=0.95 R=0.88)
  PASS  PHI redactor: 18 Safe Harbor + 3 kolm extensions, no leakage
  PASS  Ed25519 signature, Rekor transparency log entry
  WROTE task.kolm

# 3. Verify, deploy anywhere
$ kolm verify task.kolm
  PASS  24/24 checks · Ed25519 + Sigstore validated · receipt chain intact

When Proxis alone is the right answer.

You are iterating fast on the distillation loop

You need to try a dozen teacher and student combinations in a week. A focused distillation tool with great developer ergonomics is the right shape; signing every iteration is friction you do not need yet.

Your buyer does not need a receipt chain

Cost and latency wins are the only conversation. No regulated data, no procurement-grade audit requirement, no third-party verifier needed.

You own the entire pipeline already

The same team trains, deploys, and operates. You do not need a signed handoff between build and deploy because there is no handoff.

You want a single-vendor relationship

One YC-stage vendor with a tight feedback loop is more valuable to you than a multi-vendor signed-artifact workflow.

When kolm + Proxis is the right shape.

You use Proxis for the inner distillation loop and kolm for the outer build artifact: signed manifest, K-score gate, PHI redactor, receipt chain, supersession lifecycle, third-party auditor attestation. The .kolm file is what your compliance team, your auditor, and your downstream runtime consume. Proxis owns the experimentation; kolm owns the cryptographic shipment.
