kolm  /  compare  /  kolm vs Together AI (2026)

Together is hosted training. kolm is a file you own.

Together AI is the leading independent inference and training cloud: serverless endpoints across 200+ open models, hosted LoRA fine-tuning, dedicated GPU clusters, and a strong throughput story. It is excellent at what it does. kolm does something different: it emits a signed, portable, compiled .kolm artifact that runs on your hardware and ships its own receipt chain. Both can be right; rarely the same buyer at the same moment.

Wave 274 · reviewed 2026-05-18 · hosted training vs portable signed file

What Together AI ships in 2026.

  • Serverless inference on 200+ open models (Llama, Qwen, DeepSeek, Mistral, Mixtral, Gemma, Phi, and more) with OpenAI-compatible APIs.
  • Hosted LoRA fine-tuning on managed clusters. Bring a JSONL dataset, get back a tuned model accessible via a Together endpoint.
  • Dedicated endpoints + GPU clusters. For high-volume workloads you reserve A100/H100/H200 capacity with negotiated rates.
  • Together Inference Engine. Speculative decoding, FP8 quantization, and custom kernels delivering strong tokens-per-second economics.

What Together AI does not ship: a portable signed file you mail to a phone, an air-gapped deployment without a network call to Together, a cryptographically verifiable receipt chain proving what trained the model, or a compiled native binary that runs on edge hardware.

kolm-built adapter served on Together.

The cleanest pairing: kolm produces a signed compiled adapter. You upload the LoRA weights to Together for hosted serving on their inference engine. Your auditor reads the kolm manifest and Rekor entry; your customers consume an OpenAI-compatible URL from Together. The .kolm file is the cryptographic contract between the build and the serve. 
# 1. Build the signed adapter with kolm
$ kolm distill intake-classifier \
    --teacher=anthropic:claude-opus-4-7 \
    --student-base=qwen2.5-7b \
    --redact=phi --eval-gate=0.85

  PASS  K-score 0.912 (A=0.93 T=0.92 F=0.94 R=0.89)
  PASS  PHI redactor: 18 Safe Harbor + 3 kolm extensions, no leakage
  PASS  Ed25519 signature, Rekor transparency log entry
  WROTE intake-classifier.kolm

# 2. Extract the LoRA for Together upload
$ kolm extract intake-classifier.kolm --target=lora-weights --out=./adapter

# 3. Upload to Together AI
$ together adapter upload ./adapter --base=Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B --name=intake-classifier

# 4. Verify the served adapter still matches what kolm signed
$ kolm verify intake-classifier.kolm
  PASS  24/24 checks · Ed25519 + Sigstore validated · receipt chain intact

When Together AI is the right answer.

SaaS that needs scale-from-zero serving

You want a serverless endpoint across 200+ open models with one of the strongest tokens-per-second stories in market. Together's Inference Engine is genuinely strong; running a comparable runtime yourself rarely pays off.

Hosted fine-tune, hosted serve, one vendor

You want to upload a JSONL dataset, get back a tuned model, and serve it without operating GPUs. Together collapses that workflow cleanly with one bill.

Throughput-bound workload, reserved capacity

Your token volume justifies a dedicated H100 / H200 cluster on negotiated rates. Together's dedicated endpoint SKU is designed for exactly that workload.

Model breadth is the procurement requirement

You need on-demand inference against many open models for evaluation, benchmarking, or routing experiments. Together's catalog is the broadest independent option.

When kolm is the right answer.

You ship to a phone, an edge box, a regulated VPC, or any environment where calling out to Together is a procurement problem. Your auditor needs cryptographic proof of what trained the model and that no raw PHI leaked into the adapter. Your CFO needs flat compile cost rather than per-token forever. The .kolm file is the contract; you can still serve it through Together when scale demands it.
