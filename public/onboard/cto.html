Self-host vs managed: a decision tree

Pick the branch that matches your constraints. Both branches produce identical artifacts and identical receipts. The only difference is where the control plane runs.

Self-host

  • You picked PHI or IP above.
  • You need air-gap or VPC isolation.
  • You already run Kubernetes or a hypervisor pool.
  • You want HSM-managed KMS keys.

Recommended for healthcare, defense, and large finance.

Managed

  • You picked PII or PUB only.
  • You want zero ops overhead.
  • You are okay with US-East data residency.
  • You want billing through Stripe.

Recommended for SaaS, internal tooling, and pilots.

Compile your first redactor

A redactor is the smallest unit you can ship through the receipt chain. The reference PHI redactor compiles in under five minutes on a single GPU.

$ kolm new phi-redactor --from redactor $ kolm compile --spec phi-redactor.spec.json building spec receipt. generating seeds.jsonl (100 rows). distilling against Qwen3-VL teacher. running 12-axis K-score. artifact OK. K-score: 0.94. receipt signed. $ kolm verify phi-redactor.kolm signature OK. receipt chain intact. Rekor entry: 0x7e4f...

what you get

A signed .kolm artifact, a Rekor inclusion proof, a 12-axis K-score, and a tenant-side shadow corpus hash. Drop it into any HTTP endpoint or run it on-device.
