kolm  /  receipt

Receipt verification.

Anyone with this hash can verify a receipt was issued, when, and that the HMAC chain is intact. No login. No payload data leaves a receipt by design. Tenant identity is hidden unless the customer opts in.

Looking up receipt

The 4-ring chain

Each ring is an HMAC-SHA256 over the previous ring plus the new payload. Break any byte anywhere in the chain and every ring downstream stops verifying. This is what "tamper-evident" actually means in code.

What this proves

01 . issued

The receipt was signed by kolm at the timestamp shown. The signing key is on file with our compliance package.

02 . chain intact

HMAC-SHA256 across every ring of the chain re-verified server-side. A single byte change in the artifact, eval set, or receipt body would break it.

03 . artifact unchanged

The artifact_hash above is the sha256 of the bytes in the .kolm at sign-time. Anyone can recompute it from a copy and confirm a match.

Verify offline

Same checks, in your terminal, against your local copy of the artifact. No network call, no kolm involvement.

$ kolm verify --hash <hash>
