FAQ — the real questions

"What if synthesis fails on a hard call?"

It declines. We never replace a call that doesn't pass our verification gate (default: ≥95% match rate against your real traffic). The hard 20% stays on the LLM. We only kill the easy 80%.

"Is this just caching?"

No. Caching needs the *same input* to hit. A cluster of 10,000 distinct support-ticket-classification prompts has a 0% cache rate. We synthesize a function that handles all 10,000 deterministically.

"How do you handle drift / new edge cases?"

The autopilot keeps observing. When a new cluster emerges, we synthesize again. When a deployed recipe disagrees with the LLM's recent output above a threshold, we re-train it. Always shadow-running, never silently wrong.

"Why won't OpenAI / Anthropic ship this themselves?"

Their revenue scales with calls served. Auto-replacing their calls with deterministic functions is the worst possible product for their P&L. Our entire business is the structural alignment problem they can't solve.

"Can I self-host?"

Synthesis engine + runtime are open-source. Autopilot orchestration + registry are proprietary; available on-prem for enterprise plans.
