Positioning

Kolm wins by owning trust, not tensors.

The runtime layer is being commoditized by hyperscalers and open source. Kolm becomes valuable when it is the artifact control plane developers, compliance teams, and device runtimes all trust.

Against Core ML and LiteRT

Use them as targets.

Apple and Google are runtime infrastructure. Kolm should export into them while owning registry, policy, K-score, receipts, and cross-platform release management.

Against generic AI gateways

Govern artifacts, not prompts.

Gateways route cloud calls. Kolm governs portable behavior that can run offline, personalize locally, and prove what artifact was active.

Against open-source runtimes

Make trust the network effect.

The more artifacts, evals, receipts, and private-registry policies Kolm hosts, the stronger the K-score standard becomes.

Why Kolm exists

Every competitor leaves a governance gap.

The site needs to answer the VC and developer objection directly. Kolm is not "better Core ML." Kolm is the missing artifact lifecycle around native runtimes.
