Privacy · data minimisation by design

What we keep.
What we never touch.

A short, verifiable privacy policy. Plain language first; defined terms second. The product is built so the smallest possible amount of data crosses our infrastructure.

last updated · 2026-05-07

The short version. We collect your email, a one-way hash of your kolm API key, and the metadata needed to operate compile jobs. Hosted preview users should treat submitted specs, examples, and evals as data sent to the service. Customer-hosted bridge deployments are designed to keep regulated data in customer-controlled infrastructure.

01What we collect

When you sign up, we collect your email address and an optional name. The backend issues a one-time kolm API key and stores a SHA-256 hash plus a short prefix for lookup. We do not store passwords because there are none.

When you call kolm compile, we record:

  • Job metadata: compile id, base model, gate, K-score, byte counts, wall-clock duration, success/failure.
  • Hashes: SHA-256 of your task prompt, examples set, evals set, and the resulting artifact.
  • Submitted compile material: task specs, examples, evals, and generated receipts needed to build and verify the artifact.

When you visit kolm.ai, our edge logs your IP, user-agent, and request path for 30 days for abuse protection. We do not run third-party analytics, ad pixels, or session replay.

02What we never collect

  • Your production training corpus by default. Regulated source data should stay in customer-controlled storage unless a customer-hosted bridge has been contracted and deployed.
  • Your inference inputs and outputs. Recipe-mode CLI runs can stay local after download. Native model and recall-index runtimes must be verified per deployment before broader offline claims are made.
  • Your frontier API key. Provider keys are intended to be pass-through and not persisted by kolm. Deployment logging and provider behavior should be reviewed before regulated use.
  • Your artifact contents. Current public artifacts include recipes, evals, manifests, receipts, and placeholder model/index slots. Customer storage and full model bundles depend on deployment configuration.

03Where it lives

Account records and job metadata live in the deployment's configured data directory or backing store. Production deployments must set durable KOLM_DATA_DIR and KOLM_ARTIFACT_DIR locations before readiness passes. Third-party processors depend on the deployment and are reviewed by contract.

Customer-hosted Enterprise deployments can run the compile pipeline inside customer-controlled infrastructure once the bridge is contracted and deployed.

04How long it stays

  • Job metadata: today retained indefinitely; the 90-day aggregate-and-purge job is target architecture and not yet running.
  • Edge access logs: 30 days, then purged.
  • Account records: kept while your account is open. Today POST /v1/account/delete performs a soft delete: the tenant row is marked _deleted: true, the session cookie clears, and the API key stops authorizing. Concepts, versions, observations, invocations, compile jobs, and artifact zips remain in storage. Full purge within 30 days is target architecture; for now, regulated deployments should use the customer-hosted bridge or contact us for an operator-run purge.
  • Recipe contributions: retained according to the registry configuration for that deployment. Public contribution should be opt-in and reviewed for sensitive workflows.

05Your rights

You can:

  • Export your account and job metadata by emailing dev@kolm.ai; an automated kolm account export CLI is target architecture.
  • Deactivate your account via POST /v1/account/delete: API key is revoked, billing cancels, session clears. Full deletion (concepts/versions/observations/invocations/compile jobs/artifacts) is target architecture; ask for an operator-run purge if you need it before that ships.
  • Object to recipe registry contributions on a per-job basis with the --no-contribute flag at compile time.

EU and UK residents have GDPR rights on the categories above. California residents have CCPA rights. Both are honored against the same email.

06Cookies

We set one cookie: kolm_session. It is httpOnly, Secure, SameSite=Lax, and bound to your account. We do not use cookies for tracking, analytics, or advertising.

07Children

kolm is for adults. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 16.

08Changes

We will email every active account at least 14 days before any material change to this policy. Past versions are archived at github.com/sneaky-hippo/kolmogorov-stack.

09Contact

Privacy questions, GDPR requests, CCPA requests: dev@kolm.ai. Security disclosures: dev@kolm.ai. General: dev@kolm.ai.
