01baa · what it covers

HIPAA business-associate agreement.

In the kolm architecture, PHI stays where you put it the covered entity's network at run time. The artifact runs inside your boundary. The BAA exists to cover the narrow window where kolm acts as a business associate: support engagements, optional capture-and-distill against your training data, and any incident-response touch points. The template below is the standard kolm BAA, annotated.

Definitions“Covered Entity” is your organization. “Business Associate” is kolm. “PHI” tracks 45 CFR 160.103. We do not redefine standard terms.
Permitted uses & disclosureskolm uses PHI only to provide the services you contracted for. Aggregated, de-identified analytics permitted only with your explicit opt-in, and the de-identification standard is 45 CFR 164.514(b)(2) (Safe Harbor) by default.
SafeguardsAdministrative, physical, technical safeguards per 45 CFR 164.308–312. Receipt-chain integrity is product-level; the BAA covers the people-and-process layer.
Subcontractorskolm names every sub-processor in Schedule 2 (DPA section). At time of writing: Vercel (static hosting, no PHI), Railway (API hosting, encrypted at rest, no PHI by design), Stripe (billing only). All under BAA where they touch PHI; none do under the default architecture.
Breach notificationkolm notifies you within 24 hours of discovery. Investigation report within 5 business days. We do not negotiate “reasonable delay” clauses.
Term and terminationBAA is coterminous with the master agreement. On termination, kolm returns or destroys PHI within 30 days and certifies destruction in writing. No retention “for archival” exceptions.
02dpa · what it covers

Data-processing addendum (GDPR-aligned).

For EU subject data, the same template covers GDPR processor obligations under Article 28. Schedule 1 lists processing categories, subject categories, retention. Schedule 3 covers transfer mechanism (SCCs incorporated by reference; UK addendum on request).

RolesCustomer is Controller. kolm is Processor. We do not become Controller of your data under any clause.
Processing instructionskolm processes Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer. The product itself enforces this: there is no off-product training on your data without opt-in.
Sub-processors (Schedule 2)Same list as BAA. We notify of additions 30 days before activation; you may object in writing.
International transfers (Schedule 3)EU SCCs (Module 2: Controller-to-Processor) incorporated by reference. UK IDTA addendum available on request. Default deployment can be EU-only on request.
Data subject requestskolm assists Controller in responding to access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection requests within 7 business days.
Audit rightsOne audit per year on 30 days notice, conducted under reasonable confidentiality.
03what we will not negotiate

The lines we hold.

A short list of clauses we do not redline. Saying it up front saves a week of back-and-forth.

Indemnity capCapped at 12 months of fees paid. Standard. We will not accept uncapped indemnity for IP, data, or anything else.
Source-code escrowNot available at current stage. We will publish the .kolm format spec (already public at /spec) and commit to format stability instead.
Source-code reviewAvailable under NDA for design-partner engagements. Not generally.
On-prem control-planeThe runtime is already local. The control plane (key issue, billing) stays kolm-hosted at current stage. Air-gap reference deployment is documented at /edge for orgs that need 100% on-prem.
SLA > 99.9%Control plane only. Runtime SLA is your local hardware. We will not sign 99.99% on the control plane until our infra is multi-region with autofailover.
04schedule 1 · phi schedule

PHI Schedule (Schedule 1 to the BAA).

This Schedule is attached to and incorporated into the executed BAA. It is the operative document for any engagement where Protected Health Information may transit a kolm-controlled boundary — including support, capture-and-distill against PHI seed data, and incident response. Plain language; legal force.

Purposekolm acts as a Business Associate of Covered Entity solely to provide the kolm compile, run, and audit services contracted under the master agreement. Nothing in this Schedule permits use of PHI for product improvement, model training across tenants, marketing, or any purpose not enumerated in §2 (Permitted Uses).
Permitted Uses(a) Compile PHI seed data submitted by Covered Entity into a customer-owned .kolm artifact within the customer's hosted bridge or kolm's HIPAA-eligible boundary. (b) Run the resulting artifact at Covered Entity's direction. (c) Maintain audit and receipt logs containing only metadata (sha256, K-score, timestamp, tenant ID) — no PHI payload. (d) Respond to Covered Entity's support requests where PHI may be incidentally observed; kolm does not retain copies beyond the support window.
Safeguardskolm implements: (a) Customer-hosted compile bridge (BYOC) so PHI stays inside the customer-hosted bridge the Covered Entity VPC at compile time. (b) PHI-redactor recipe applied before any frontier-model call, redacting SSN, NPI, MRN, DOB, phone, email, and address by default. (c) K-score gate of ≥0.95 for any artifact tagged phi=true (vs the 0.85 baseline). (d) HMAC-SHA256 receipt chain (4 rings) sealing each compile and run, with break-detection on tamper. (e) AES-256 encryption at rest with customer-managed keys (CMK) on request; TLS 1.3 in transit. (f) Role-based access control with quarterly review and immediate revocation on offboarding.
Breach Notificationkolm notifies Covered Entity within 10 business days of discovery of any Breach of Unsecured PHI, in accordance with 45 CFR 164.410. Initial notice within 24 hours of discovery (operational best-effort); formal written notice and incident report within 10 business days. kolm does not contract for “reasonable delay” provisions. Notification is sent to the Covered Entity privacy officer of record listed on the executed BAA cover page.
Subprocessors & Flow-Downkolm flows down all material terms of this BAA to any subprocessor that may access PHI. The current subprocessor inventory is published at /subprocessors and updated within 1 business day of any change. By design, only the customer-hosted bridge handles PHI under the default architecture; Vercel, Railway, Stripe, Resend, Cloudflare, and GitHub do not receive PHI. Frontier vendors (Anthropic / OpenAI / Google) receive seed examples only at compile time and only when the customer has explicitly opted in with their own key.

Flow-down for opt-in subprocessors: When Covered Entity chooses to enable Anthropic / OpenAI / Google by providing their own API key, the contractual relationship is between Covered Entity and the vendor directly. Covered Entity is responsible for executing its own BAA with that vendor where required. kolm passes inputs through unchanged after redaction by the customer-hosted bridge; kolm does not act as an intermediary BAA conduit for those vendors. Example flow: Acme Health is the Covered Entity; Acme has a BAA with Anthropic directly; kolm's bridge redacts PHI before any payload reaches Anthropic; the kolm tenant carries no PHI through its own subprocessor inventory.
Return / DestructionUpon termination of the master agreement, or earlier on written request, kolm returns or destroys all PHI in its possession within 30 days and provides written certification of destruction. Where return or destruction is infeasible (e.g. PHI fragments in long-term archival receipts), kolm extends the protections of this Schedule to such PHI indefinitely and limits further use to the purposes that make return or destruction infeasible. Customer-owned .kolm artifacts are not affected; they remain in the customer's possession.
Audit RightsCovered Entity may audit kolm's compliance with this Schedule once per year on 30 days' written notice, during business hours, under reasonable confidentiality. Audit may include review of policies, training records, access logs (redacted of other-tenant data), incident records, and subprocessor inventories. Covered Entity bears the cost of the audit unless material non-compliance is discovered, in which case kolm reimburses reasonable audit fees. Cause-based audits (e.g. following a Breach) are exempt from the 30-day notice and the annual cap.
Mitigation & Cooperationkolm mitigates, to the extent practicable, any harmful effect of a use or disclosure of PHI by kolm in violation of this Schedule. kolm cooperates with Covered Entity's investigation, breach analysis, and notification obligations to individuals, HHS, and (where required) media.
HIPAA Security Rule Mappingkolm maps controls to the HIPAA Security Rule administrative (45 CFR 164.308), physical (45 CFR 164.310), technical (45 CFR 164.312), organizational (45 CFR 164.314), and documentation (45 CFR 164.316) safeguards in a separate control-mapping document available at /hipaa-mapping, refreshed quarterly. SOC 2 Type II audit is in progress with target attestation Q4 2026; SOC 2 Type I scoping completed 2026-04. HITRUST CSF certification scoping in progress; target r2 attestation Q2 2027.
SurvivalThe obligations of confidentiality, return-or-destruction, breach notification (for incidents discovered post-termination), and audit (for the preceding 12 months) survive termination of the master agreement.

Capitalized terms not defined in this Schedule have the meaning given in the BAA or the master agreement. In any conflict, this Schedule controls for PHI-related matters; the master agreement controls otherwise.
