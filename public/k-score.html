04 ·The gate

One number per artifact. It decides if that artifact ships.

Per-artifact, not per-model. Every .kolm file carries its own single decimal, K, computed against its own frozen eval set. phi-redactor.kolm has one K. triage.kolm has its own. Neither is a leaderboard for the base model underneath. Below the gate, kolm refuses to write the file. No eval, no ship.

Definition K = wa·Accuracy + ws·Size_inv + wl·Latency_inv + wc·Cost_inv + wv·Coverage
where ∑ w = 1

(Weighted average of five measurable qualities, normalized against the frontier model this artifact was distilled from.)

Components are normalized to [0,1] against the frontier baseline this artifact was distilled from. 1.0 means the artifact ties or beats frontier on every component for its task. 0.0 means it doesn't exist. Two artifacts both scoring 0.94 are each ship-ready against their own eval set; they are not "equivalently good" because the eval sets differ.

01 ·Components

Five inputs. One number.

No mystery. Every component is reproducible from the artifact and the tests it was sealed with. Anyone with the file can re-run the math.

w ·0.40
a
Accuracy
Pass-rate on tests.jsonl, the eval suite stored in the artifact. The dominant term.
w ·0.15
s
Size
Inverse-normalized against base. Smaller is closer to 1. A 38 MB lift is a 0.95.
w ·0.15
l
Latency
p50 ms / token. Locally measured. 80 ms vs frontier 1240 ms = 0.94.
w ·0.15
c
Cost
$/1k runs. Local-only artifacts hit 1.0; cloud-pointer hybrids drop proportionally.
w ·0.15
v
Coverage
Share of expected outputs covered by the recipe pack. Drives speculative-decoding speedup.
02 ·The gate

0.85 ships. Anything below fails closed.

- nbsp;0.85
Default ship gate. Compile fails closed if K < 0.85; the artifact never gets a signature. Tune with kolm compile --gate 0.95 for safety-critical tasks, or relax with --gate 0.70 for prototypes. The threshold is part of the manifest, anchored to the receipt, immutable post-sign.

04 ·Why one number

Because shipping is binary.

Every model card in the wild today is a tasteful menu of cherry-picked plots. You read down the page and never decide. K is the decision.

Every component is recoverable, but the gate hides the wiring. A green K means this artifact passed its own tests, fits on the device, runs faster than frontier, costs nothing to invoke, and the recipe pack covers most of the outputs it’ll be asked for. A single decimal lets a CI pipeline gate a deploy. Lets a registry rank artifacts within the same task. Lets a phone decide whether to download. The K travels with the file; pick up the artifact, you pick up its score.

If you don'like 0.85, override it. The number is the contract; the threshold is the policy. They're separate on purpose.
