How the loop closes

StepWhat happens
1. CaptureYour client hits kolm.ai/v1/capture/{openai,anthropic}. We forward upstream, return the response, and record (model, prompt, output, status, latency, ts, namespace) under your tenant. Send the namespace via x-kolm-namespace; the inbox surface and the distill counter share the same namespace field.
2. TriageKeep: mark for export. Discard: hide from the inbox and exclude from /v1/labels/synthesize-corpus and /v1/specialists/auto-distill. Promote: deep-link into the recipe playground to lift one observation into a reusable spec.
3. DistillAt 1,000 kept captures in a namespace, the namespace flips to ready. One click fires POST /v1/specialists/auto-distill; the route hands the job to the configured trainer bridge that owns the fit, scoring, and signing, then returns a signed .kolm Specialist back to your account.
4. SwitchRe-point the client at your own kolm serve. Per-call cost drops to electricity. Each .kolm artifact carries an HMAC-SHA256 signature over its archive; each invocation returns a _kolm run metadata block the issuer or any holder of the shared tenant secret can re-verify.

What we never persist

Your upstream provider key (forwarded in x-upstream-api-key, dropped after the call). Cross-tenant data: the inbox is scoped to your tenant id by your API key. Captured prompts and responses are stored on the tenant row until you delete them via the inbox or your account is soft-deleted.
