AI compiler comparison: kolm vs Predibase vs OpenPipe vs LoRAX

Five tools commonly show up in evaluations for "turn our captured prompts into a deployable model": kolm.ai, Predibase (acquired by Rubrik June 2025), OpenPipe, LoRAX, and the DIY stack of LLaMA-Factory + Axolotl + Unsloth. This is the 10-axis side-by-side. The matrix is followed by a "where each is right" section because no one of these is the right answer for every workload.

Where each is the right answer

Predibase / Rubrik is the right answer when

Your procurement requires a Series-C-or-later vendor with a Big-Four-flavored governance story. You serve many LoRA adapters at high QPS in one cluster (Predibase's serving runtime is mature for this). The Rubrik 2025 acquisition aligned the product with an enterprise governance buyer who prefers a single procurement contact across security + AI.

OpenPipe is the right answer when

You want capture + fine-tune + hosted endpoint as a single managed service and you do not want the artifact off the platform. The OpenPipe loop is the fastest path from "wrap an existing call" to "served fine-tune at lower per-token cost" if you accept the hosted-endpoint deployment shape.

LoRAX is the right answer when

You already have LoRA adapters (from any source) and you need multi-tenant serving throughput with adapter swapping in a single process. LoRAX is not a competitor; it is a complement. kolm.ai compiles an artifact and (optionally) exports a LoRA suitable for LoRAX serving. See /how-vs-lorax for the stack diagram.

DIY is the right answer when

You have a research team that wants total control of the training loop and the existing tools (LLaMA-Factory, Axolotl, Unsloth, TRL, PEFT) are already in your environment. The price of total control is you own everything kolm.ai automates: the eval gate, the redactor, the receipt chain, the drift detection, the supersession discipline, the cross-vendor teacher mediation. If your team has the capacity for that, DIY is honest. See /how-vs-diy.

kolm.ai is the right answer when

You want capture-to-artifact-to-audit-to-deploy as one tool, with the artifact under your control, the audit posture good enough for regulated buyers, and the lifecycle (eval gate + drift + supersession) operated by the same tool. The shape kolm.ai was built for: regulated workloads, edge / on-device, ship-to-customer hardware, defense, and procurement contexts where the buyer needs to verify the model without trusting any third party.

Migration paths

From OpenPipe to kolm

kolm import openpipe ./openpipe-export.jsonl --to=my-namespace
kolm distill --from-captures my-namespace --teacher=anthropic
kolm compile my-namespace --tier=3090
kolm verify my-namespace.kolm

From Predibase to kolm

predibase export adapter --id=<id> --format=safetensors --out=./adapter
kolm import predibase-adapter ./adapter --base=qwen3-27b-dense
kolm compile my-namespace --tier=3090
kolm verify my-namespace.kolm

From DIY to kolm

# If you already trained with LLaMA-Factory or Axolotl
kolm import safetensors ./checkpoint --recipe ./recipe.toml --eval ./frozen-eval.jsonl
kolm compile --tier=3090
kolm verify my-namespace.kolm
Honest framing. "kolm wins on 8 of 10 axes" is true on the matrix above; the matrix axes were chosen because they are the axes regulated and on-device buyers ask about. A different audience (a SaaS team picking the cheapest hosted fine-tune for an internal experiment) cares about different axes and would draw a different conclusion. Use the right-answer section above as the honest read.
