Security policy

How kolm protects you.

Plain-language summary of what we encrypt, where it lives, who can read it, and how to report a vulnerability.

What we hold

The minimum needed to run your compiles.

  • Email + password hash (scrypt, memory-hard).
  • Your model API keys, encrypted with AES-256-GCM at rest. Decrypted only inside the compile process, never logged.
  • Your recall corpus, encrypted at rest in our managed index. Free / Mobile / Pro can opt in to on-device-only with sqlite-vec.
  • Compile receipts (HMAC chain over artifact hashes — no payload).

We do not hold: your model bills, your raw prompts after compile, the contents of your .kolm artifacts after delivery.

How we sign

Every .kolm artifact ships an HMAC-SHA256 chain that anchors:

  • The base model SHA-256.
  • The LoRA delta SHA-256.
  • The recipe pack SHA-256 + RS-1 version.
  • The recall index SHA-256.
  • The compile manifest hash.
  • The signing timestamp.

Verification is local, offline, and free. Any tampered byte breaks the chain and the artifact refuses to load. See Receipts spec.

Transport

All traffic to kolm.ai uses TLS 1.3 with HSTS. The CLI pins our public key for /v1/compile and /v1/recall.

Responsible disclosure

If you believe you've found a vulnerability, please email us before publishing. We respond within 24 hours and will credit you publicly (with permission) once fixed.

  • In scope: kolm.ai, *.kolm.ai, the CLI, every published SDK, the registry signing chain.
  • Out of scope: social engineering, denial-of-service against shared infra, and anything that requires an authenticated account on someone else's tenant.
  • Bounty: we pay $250–$5,000 for valid reports, scaled by severity.

Report a vulnerability

Account deletion

From /account you can delete your account and cascade-delete every artifact, recall vector, and receipt. The wipe is irreversible. Refunds for the current billing period are pro-rated.

Data location

Compute and storage live in us-east-1 by default. Pro and Enterprise tiers can pin to eu-west-1 or ap-southeast-1; Enterprise can also run the full stack on-prem.
