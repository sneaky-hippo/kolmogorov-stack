Go-to-market

Three phases.
One compiler shape.

kolm is to AI tasks what a compiler is to code — the missing layer between a prompt and production. That framing earns its keep across three different motions: a developer who wants free + fast, a regulated buyer who wants a pilot, and an OEM who wants per-device royalty math. Same product. Same artifact. Three doors.

why now
Frontier models stopped being a moat. Per-token economics broke at production scale. Sovereignty stopped being optional in regulated work. The buyer-side response is the same one C compilers gave the 1970s: convert intent into a portable artifact you own, run it where you need it, ship one signed file across the fleet.

Phase 03 - Edge / embedded OEM

The compiler
on every device.

Industrial controllers. Medical devices. Automotive. Robotics. The shape of intelligence that runs there is small, signed, and offline-first. kolm meets that shape natively. OEM licensing and per-device royalty become the long-tail moat.

target
Industrial, medical-device, automotive, robotics OEMs.
motion
OEM licensing. Royalty per device. Reference HW partner.
goal
Volume. Hardware-anchored moat.
unit
Devices shipped with a signed .kolm on board.
Move 01
One file. Every architecture.
A .kolm that compiles on a workstation runs on an ARM Cortex-A77, a Snapdragon 8 Gen 3, an Apple M-series, and an x86_64 server. Same artifact. Same signature. No retarget.
Move 02
OEM licensing model.
Per-device royalty. Customer-controlled signing key. Optional white-label of the runtime so the OEM keeps brand on the device.
Move 03
Hardware-partner moat.
Each reference HW partner widens the moat. The artifact format becomes the lingua franca between AI teams and embedded teams — the way a compiled binary became the lingua franca between language designers and chip designers.

Why these three phases stack instead of compete.

Phase 01 generates the registry depth and benchmark diversity Phase 02 references in pilot conversations. Phase 02 generates the production-grade evidence Phase 03 references in OEM contracts. Phase 03 generates the volume that makes the marginal cost of every Phase 01 compile lower — and the loop closes. Same compiler. Same artifact. Same signature. Three doors that share a hallway.
