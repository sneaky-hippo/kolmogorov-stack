kolm  /  compare  /  how kolm relates to Predibase

Predibase serves LoRAs on their cluster. kolm ships a file.

Predibase (acquired by Rubrik in June 2025) is hosted LoRAX serving + Turbo LoRA inference + a fine-tuning UX + an enterprise tier. Post-acquisition they're pivoting toward "agentic AI governance" — agent action monitoring and rewinding. That's an adjacent category to kolm, not a competitive one: kolm sits on the build side and emits a portable signed file that lives anywhere.

Wave 169 · reviewed 2026-05-17 · build-side vs serve-side

What Predibase ships in 2026.

  • Hosted fine-tuning on managed serverless infrastructure. Free serverless tier (1M tokens/day, 10M tokens/month). Developer + Enterprise SKUs with per-second GPU billing.
  • LoRAX serving — the same Apache-2.0 multi-LoRA infra they open-sourced, deployed on Predibase cloud (or your VPC under Enterprise contract).
  • Turbo LoRA — 2-3× faster inference claim over baseline LoRAX.
  • Rubrik integration (June 2025+) — "agentic AI governance" repositioning toward agent action monitoring and rewinding. The Rubrik enterprise-security brand absorbs the Predibase data-security messaging.

What Predibase doesn't ship: a portable signed file you mail to a phone, an air-gapped deployment without their runtime, a third-party-verifiable receipt chain showing what was used to train, a cryptographically-signed eval gate on the build artifact, or a compiled native binary.

The Rubrik acquisition changed the category, not the gap.

June 2025: Rubrik (NYSE: RBRK) acquired Predibase. The combined product positioning shifted toward "agentic AI governance" — monitoring agent actions, rewinding bad state, applying Rubrik's data-security posture to LLM workloads.

This validated the category that kolm sits adjacent to: enterprise security positioning on top of LoRA serving is a real market. It did not close the gap that motivates kolm. Rubrik+Predibase governs the runtime; kolm proves the build. An agent action monitor doesn't tell you what teacher distilled the model, what data the train set held, whether the eval gate fired, or whether the bytes on disk match the bytes signed at compile time. Those are kolm's surface.

The two products work together well: ship a kolm-signed adapter, deploy via Predibase, let Rubrik govern the runtime. The signed file is the contract between the build artifact and the runtime governance.

When Predibase is the right answer.

Procurement wants a vendor with a Series B/C check, a dedicated VPC SKU, and a sales team backed by Rubrik. Your workload is multi-tenant adapter serving at scale and you need someone else to own the GPU operations. The Rubrik agentic-governance story matches your buyer's security narrative.

When kolm is the right answer.

Your team can consume a signed file and run it themselves, your workload is edge or air-gapped, your auditor needs cryptographic provenance on the build, or your compliance team specifically asked for the receipt chain. The PHI redactor (W157), cross-vendor distillation (W158), and supersession chain (W167) become load-bearing the moment your buyer asks "what trained this model and how do I prove it didn't see raw PHI."
