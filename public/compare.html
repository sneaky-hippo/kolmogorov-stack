kolm vs LoRAX

LoRAX is Apache-2.0 multi-LoRA serving infrastructure. kolm builds the adapter; LoRAX serves it. Complement, not competitor — the teardown shows the stack with both.

When the competitors are the right answer.

A defensible moat doesn't mean every workload should switch. Three honest cases where you should stay where you are:

stay with hyperscaler

You don't need the artifact

If your only deploy target is a hosted API, your data isn't regulated, and your buyers don't ask for compile-time receipts, the hyperscaler tuning UX is the shortest path. kolm's compliance moat is dead weight for unregulated production workloads.

stay with LoRAX

You serve thousands of adapters per GPU

LoRAX's heterogeneous continuous batching and SGMV kernel are real engineering. kolm doesn't compete on multi-tenant serving throughput. Pair the two: kolm builds the signed adapter, LoRAX serves it at scale.

stay with Predibase

Procurement needs a Series-C vendor

Predibase + Rubrik (acquired June 2025) is a known enterprise security brand with a dedicated VPC SKU and a sales team. If your buyer's procurement asks "headquartered where, what's MRR," that's a strong fit. kolm sits in the build-side category; the two are adjacent, not identical.

stay with OpenPipe

You want hosted inference, not a file

OpenPipe's capture-then-fine-tune SDK is a clean path to a hosted endpoint. kolm captures the same way but ships a file. If you don't need the file, the endpoint is one fewer thing to operate.

Ship private AI as an artifact, not a promise.

Start with the compiler. Prove the artifact. Then choose whether to serve it on LoRAX, on a phone, or in an air-gapped VPC. The .kolm file is the same in all three.
