step 01 · compile

The model is born inside your boundary.

kolm reads a JSON spec plus your gold examples and produces a single .kolm file. The compile runs on your hardware. The bytes never leave the host.

The K-score is the gate. 0.85 is the default floor. CI exits non-zero on a fail so the artifact never gets promoted by accident.

no cloud roundtrip · no training data leak
step 02 · verify

The artifact carries its own proof.

Every .kolm has a SHA-256, an HMAC-signed receipt chain, and the K-score it passed at. Inspection is offline. You don't need our infrastructure to trust the file.

The signature also pins the recipe source and the spec evals: if anything inside the artifact changes, the signature breaks.

verifiable offline · signature breaks on tamper
step 03 · publish

Share with the team. Nobody else.

Publish with --team <handle> and the artifact is owned by the team, not a person. Members pull it. Non-members get 404. We don't even confirm the name exists.

Storage and bandwidth are billed on the Teams plan ($149/mo, 5 seats). Visibility defaults to private; --public is opt-in.

private by default · 5 seats · $149/mo
step 04 · audit

Receipts your auditor will accept.

Each pull and each run appends to a receipt chain. The K-score is checked against the publish-time value to detect drift. External calls are counted (and forbidden by default).

One quarterly PDF rolls this up: regression report, drift, sub-processor delta, incident log. Goes to your auditor unchanged.

drift = 0 · egress = 0 · auditor-ready

04what a pilot looks like

Eight weeks. Three artifacts.

No vague “explore the technology together” pilot. Every design-partner engagement has the same shape: a kickoff scoped to one workload, a compiled artifact under K-score 0.85 by week 4, a production-shadow deployment by week 6, a sign-off package by week 8. If your team cannot commit a security reviewer and a workload owner, this is not the right time.

week 0 · kickoffEngagement letter + mutual NDA + MSA. One workload picked. Gold-example collection plan agreed.
week 1–3 · gold examplesYour team supplies 80–300 labeled examples. We co-author the K-score axis weights and ship floor for your domain.
week 4 · first compileCompiled .kolm meets your K-score floor on hold-out test. Signed receipt chain demoed end-to-end.
week 5–6 · shadow runArtifact deployed shadow-mode behind your current pipeline. Outputs compared, regressions called out.
week 7 · production gateWorkload owner + security reviewer sign. Artifact promoted to primary. Old pipeline kept as fallback for one week.
week 8 · sign-off packageYou receive: signed artifact, K-score audit report, receipt-chain integrity proof, sub-processor list, MSA executed. Compliance binder Q1 begins next quarter.
