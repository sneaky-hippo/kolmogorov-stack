02How it works

You compile in your VPC.
You ship to the device.

The Enterprise design target is a customer-hosted compile bridge and a signed artifact that can be reviewed before deployment. The public product should not be treated as a HIPAA compliance certification.

-

BAA review path.

Healthcare deployments need contract review before PHI is in scope. Default-redacted logs and retention controls are deployment requirements, not public certification.

-

Customer-hosted bridge design.

A bridge that runs inside customer-controlled infrastructure is the Enterprise design path. It is reviewed per deployment.

-

HMAC-chained receipts.

Every output is reproducible. Every layer is content-addressed. Tampering with any layer breaks the chain. Useful at audit, defensible at deposition.

03Use case

Reference deployment:
clinical-note assistant.

Reference target: a signed artifact for clinical note drafting, reviewed on de-identified examples first, then evaluated against held-out cases before any production workflow.

compile pilot · customer-controlled environment · signed artifact

From institutional examples to a signed binary on the tablet.

Compile happens once per quarter against the institution's de-identified gold set. The artifact is signed, K-scored, and pushed to the MDM for distribution. Deployment is a single signed file, not a service.

01 - gather

Examples + evals

De-identified note pairs from the institution's gold set. Held-out evaluation drawn separately and never seen at training.

02 - compile

kolm compile

Synthesize a verifier from your examples. Specialize a small model on your data. Index your private context. Sign. K-score gate enforces the ship floor.

03 - review

Clinical sign-off

Hold-out evaluation report goes to the clinical informatics committee. K-score breakdown is part of the artifact, defensible at audit.

04 - ship

Push to device

Single signed file deploys via MDM. Runtime is offline. Receipts mirror to your audit log via HL7 or webhook on demand.

05Payer use-cases

Production patterns for
health insurance.

Each workflow below is a signed .kolm artifact that runs in your environment or on the workstation. PHI, claims data, and benefit-plan docs stay inside the auditor's boundary. Every output ships with a receipt that ties the model version, the input hash, and the K-score to the determination.

-

Prior authorization triage.

Inbound PA requests are classified against benefit-plan policy and InterQual/MCG criteria. The artifact drafts a determination letter with policy citations and routes edge cases to a nurse reviewer. Receipt names the policy version it cited.

-

Claims adjudication assist.

Extracts structured fields from CMS-1500 / UB-04, checks against the plan's benefit grid and coding edits, flags adjudication blockers before they hit pend queue. Member ID and claim line never leave the boundary.

-

Appeals & grievances drafts.

Drafts member-facing appeal responses against your medical policy library, cites the plan section and the CMS or state rule, and emits a defensible audit trail for the regulator. Same artifact your appeals analyst signs off on.

-

Member services AI.

Answers benefit questions from your SBC/EOC documents on the rep's workstation. Never sends member PHI to a frontier API. K-score gate enforces a deflection-rate floor before the artifact reaches a member-facing channel.

-

Utilization management review.

Summarizes medical-necessity packets for the UM reviewer: relevant chart sections, prior denials, applicable criteria, and the policy delta. The reviewer signs; kolm logs which artifact version produced the summary.

-

FWA pattern detection.

Flags suspicious claim patterns (upcoded E/M visits, duplicate-day services, ineligible provider) against the plan's edit set. Every trigger is a signed receipt that an SIU investigator can re-run byte-identically against the same claim record.

Designed for payer teams running on TPAs, MA / D-SNP plans, ACOs, and risk-bearing entities. Each pattern is a starting artifact, recompiled against your formulary, your policy library, and your eligibility feed. Provision Enterprise →
